The PepperHeads Heat Scale

What are Scoville Heat Units (SHU)?

Developed by Wilbur Scoville in 1912, the Scoville Scale measures the concentration of capsaicinoids in peppers. Originally a taste test, it's now measured with high-performance liquid chromatography for accuracy.

Mild (1,000-10,000 SHU)

Jalapeño, Poblano, Anaheim

Medium (10,000-100,000 SHU)

Serrano, Cayenne, Thai Bird's Eye

Hot (100,000-350,000 SHU)

Habanero, Scotch Bonnet, Fatalii

Super Hot (350,000+ SHU)

Ghost Pepper, Trinidad Scorpion, Carolina Reaper

Comprehensive Hot Pepper Varieties Guide

Jalapeño (3,500-8,000 SHU)

Popular Mexican pepper with thick walls, perfect for stuffing or pickling. Turns red when fully ripe.

Poblano/Ancho (1,000-2,500 SHU)

Large, heart-shaped pepper with mild heat. Called Poblano when fresh, Ancho when dried.

Anaheim (500-2,500 SHU)

Long, slender pepper with very mild heat. Great for roasting and stuffing.

Hungarian Wax (3,500-8,000 SHU)

Candle-shaped pepper that ripens from yellow to red. Similar heat to jalapeños.

Serrano (10,000-23,000 SHU)

Small, cylindrical Mexican pepper with bright, crisp heat. Excellent for salsas and sauces.

Cayenne (30,000-50,000 SHU)

Thin, wrinkled pepper commonly dried and ground into powder. Versatile for many cuisines.

Thai Bird's Eye (50,000-100,000 SHU)

Small, pointed peppers with intense heat. Essential in Southeast Asian cooking.

Tabasco (30,000-50,000 SHU)

Small, pointed pepper used in the famous hot sauce. Juicy with thin walls.

Habanero (100,000-350,000 SHU)

Lantern-shaped Caribbean pepper with fruity flavor and intense heat.

Scotch Bonnet (100,000-350,000 SHU)

Similar to habanero but with a sweeter profile. Essential in Caribbean cuisine.

Fatalii (125,000-325,000 SHU)

African pepper with citrusy flavor and significant heat. Yellow when ripe.

Datil (100,000-300,000 SHU)

Sweet, fruity pepper from St. Augustine, Florida with habanero-level heat.

Ghost Pepper/Bhut Jolokia (800,000-1,041,000 SHU)

Former world record holder from India with slow-building, intense heat.

Trinidad Scorpion (1,200,000-2,000,000 SHU)

Distinctive "tail" and fruity flavor with extreme heat from Trinidad.

Carolina Reaper (1,400,000-2,200,000 SHU)

Current world record holder with bumpy texture and scorpion-like tail.

7 Pot/7 Pod (1,000,000-1,350,000 SHU)

Named for ability to flavor seven pots of stew with one pepper. Extremely hot.
